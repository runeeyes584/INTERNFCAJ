# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day”

### Event Objectives

- Enhance skills in applying Artificial Intelligence (AI) to optimize work performance and effective personal note management methods.
- Explore the features and operations of the virtual assistant Amazon Q (Amazon Quick) for rapid Q&A and information retrieval.
- Gain an in-depth understanding of the Amazon CloudFront service, including deployment methods and budget limits to optimize infrastructure costs.
- Analyze and explain the non-determinism of Large Language Models (LLMs).
- Learn practical development experiences from the LotusHack team's 36-hour project.
- Gain insights and real-world case studies from industry professionals.

### Speakers

- **Tinh Truong** - Platform Engineer at GoTymeX
- **Anh Pham** - Cloud Consultant at G-AsiaPacific VietNam
- **Nguyen Tuan Thinh** - DevOps/Cloud Engineer at FCAJ
- **DucDao** - Solutions Architect at Cloud Kinetics
- **Thao Nguyen, Mai Nguyen, Uyen Le** - GenAI Engineers at VIB
- **Vy Lam** - Senior Business Systems Analyst at VPBank

### Key Highlights

#### AI Application and Second Brain Building

- The importance of providing comprehensive context for AI to analyze and deliver accurate responses that closely align with requirements.
- Scientific methods for note-taking and organizing information (Second Brain) to support long-term learning and work.

#### Exploring Amazon Q Virtual Assistant

- Architecture and operational principles of the Amazon Q virtual assistant.
- Demos of how to apply Amazon Q to search and retrieve data quickly and efficiently.

#### Optimizing Amazon CloudFront Service

- Best practices for utilizing Amazon CloudFront to accelerate web application content delivery.
- Guidelines for configuring alert systems and cost limits (AWS Budgets/Billing Alarms) to prevent unexpected charges.

#### Non-Determinism of LLMs

- Analyzing the principles that cause AI to generate different answers despite receiving the exact same input prompt.
- Real-world demonstrations of changes in AI responses when adjusting configuration parameters.

#### LotusHack 36-Hour Hackathon Experience

- The UTMorpho team's journey of building an automated drag-and-drop product in just 16 hours of coding.
- Experiences in handling unexpected incidents (such as running out of AI usage quotas, sudden idea changes) and teamwork skills under high time pressure.

#### Real-world Industry Experience

- A summary of common errors encountered during practical system deployment that theory rarely covers.
- Affirming the 3 core elements of system design: Security, Reliability, and Scalability.

### Key Takeaways

- Mastered Prompt Engineering skills to interact effectively with AI, directly applicable to my personal Battleship game project.
- Equipped with the skills to set up cost alerts, ensuring effective financial risk management for my AWS account.
- Clearly understood the operational principles and advantages of the Multi-Agent model (coordinating multiple AIs) compared to using a single AI.
- Accumulated teamwork mindset, flexible problem-solving skills, and valuable lessons from senior engineers.

### Event Experience

The FCAJ Community Day was the very first workshop kicking off my internship program at FCAJ AWS. The event offered an open networking atmosphere with intuitive and easily digestible content. I was particularly impressed by the inspiring story shared by the UTMorpho team as well as the practical experiences from speaker Vy Lam. The corporate case studies not only broadened my perspective but also helped me vividly envision the environment and the nature of a technology engineer's work in the future.
